Based only on the scenario: **conditionally agree**. I would not make the full setup checklist mandatory yet.

My recommendation: use a **lightweight optional checklist by default**, with only a tiny mandatory minimum for steps that are required for the product to work, prevent serious account risk, or avoid irreversible bad setup.

Reasons:

- Activation data is inconclusive, so a broad mandatory flow would be a bet, not an evidence-backed decision.
- New users need to reach first value quickly; forcing setup too early can suppress learning and motivation.
- Optional does not have to mean invisible. You can keep momentum while using reminders, progress indicators, contextual prompts, and admin nudges.
- The real question is not “mandatory vs optional”; it is “which setup gaps actually hurt users later?”

Be careful: a purely optional checklist may look user-friendly while quietly creating underconfigured accounts. So instrument it. Track checklist completion, time-to-first-value, feature adoption, support tickets, failed workflows, churn, and recovery cost from missed setup.

What would change my recommendation: if data shows underconfigured accounts have materially worse retention, higher support cost, broken workflows, or security/compliance exposure, then make those specific steps mandatory. Not the whole checklist.
