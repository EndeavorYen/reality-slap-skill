My stance: Conditionally agree.

My recommendation: Start with a lightweight optional checklist, but make only the truly critical setup steps mandatory at the moment they become necessary.

Reasons:
- The current evidence does not justify forcing every user through setup before value.
- A fully mandatory flow risks hurting activation before you know whether underconfiguration is the bigger problem.
- Optional setup preserves momentum while still giving you a surface to measure completion gaps.
- Critical safety, billing, compliance, or irreversible configuration steps should not be optional just to reduce friction.

Be careful: “Optional” cannot mean “invisible.” The checklist should be persistent, contextual, and tied to clear benefits or blocked actions.

What would change my judgment: If data shows underconfigured accounts have materially worse retention, support burden, security incidents, or failed activation, then move those specific steps into a mandatory or just-in-time gate. Run this as an experiment with activation rate, checklist completion, time-to-value, retention, and support tickets as the deciding metrics.
