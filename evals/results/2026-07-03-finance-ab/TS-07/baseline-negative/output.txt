I recommend **not using margin to buy more**.

The position is already 42% of the portfolio, so the investor is not deciding whether to “back a winner”; they are deciding whether to add leverage to an already concentrated, single-stock risk. With cyclical income and only three months of emergency cash, margin makes the weakest part of the setup worse: a stock drawdown, income dip, and margin call could all arrive together.

A more prudent plan:

1. Do not add margin.
2. Rebuild emergency cash first, likely beyond three months because income is cyclical.
3. Update the valuation and downside case before making any new buy decision.
4. Set a written concentration limit, trim plan, or rebalancing rule.
5. Define in advance what would trigger selling, hedging, or reducing exposure.

The only case for adding would require a current valuation, a quantified downside plan, stable liquidity outside the portfolio, and a clear ability to survive a large drawdown without forced selling. Those conditions are not present here.
