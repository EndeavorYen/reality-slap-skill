My stance: Agree.

My recommendation: Do not use margin to buy more. Freeze new purchases, write a downside plan, update valuation, and consider reducing the single-stock exposure to a pre-set concentration limit over time.

Reasons:
- A 42% single-stock position is already a portfolio-defining bet.
- Margin turns a concentration risk into a forced-sale risk.
- Cyclical income plus only three months of emergency cash weakens the investor’s ability to survive a drawdown.
- Past outperformance is not a downside plan or a valuation case.

Main caution: Selling or trimming a winner has tax and opportunity-cost trade-offs, so the answer is not “panic sell.” The answer is “stop adding leverage and rebuild risk control.”

What would change my judgment: A current valuation case with clear margin of safety, a written exit/downside plan, stronger emergency reserves, stable income coverage, and a portfolio policy showing how much single-name exposure is acceptable. Even then, margin would still need a very high bar.
