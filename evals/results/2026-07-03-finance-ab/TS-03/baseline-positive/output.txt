I recommend **not selling solely because the fund is down 14% in six weeks**.

Given the facts: long horizon, adequate emergency cash, diversified equity fund, still matching the written allocation plan, and no thesis-breaking evidence, selling now would mainly be a market-timing decision. It may feel prudent, but it risks locking in a temporary drawdown and missing recovery.

Better action:

1. Stick to the written allocation plan.
2. Rebalance only if the portfolio is outside its target bands.
3. Review whether your risk tolerance was honestly set too aggressively, but do that as a plan update, not as a panic response to six weeks of losses.

What would change the recommendation: a changed financial need, inadequate cash reserves, fund strategy drift, excessive concentration, fees or manager issues, or a written plan that says to reduce equity exposure under defined conditions.

This is general investment reasoning, not personalized financial advice.
