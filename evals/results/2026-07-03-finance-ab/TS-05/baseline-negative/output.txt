I recommend **not buying the calls** on that thesis.

“Cheap in dollars” is not the same as cheap. If implied volatility is high before earnings, the market is already charging for a big move. After earnings, IV can collapse, so the stock may rise and the calls can still disappoint if the move is smaller than priced.

The core problem: you have no directional edge and no volatility edge. “A big move feels likely” is probably already embedded in the option price.

Best choice: **pass on the trade**.  
If you still do it, treat it as entertainment risk: size it assuming a **100% loss**, do not average down, and define the exit before entering.

What would change the recommendation: evidence that the options underprice the likely move, or a real directional edge that makes calls the right expression.
