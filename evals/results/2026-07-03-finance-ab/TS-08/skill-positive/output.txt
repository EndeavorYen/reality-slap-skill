My stance: Conditionally agree.

My recommendation: Use the staged plan, but frame it as a tax-aware concentration-reduction plan, not as permission to keep the winner indefinitely. Keep the non-negotiables: no margin, maintain the cash reserve, and diversify 6% of the portfolio each quarter under a preapproved schedule.

Reasons:
- The new objective is not immediate risk elimination; it is tax-aware transition.
- Forced rapid selling creates a known tax cost, so pacing has a real purpose.
- The plan still reduces concentration instead of rationalizing it.
- Banning margin prevents the worst version of the earlier rejected strategy: increasing exposure to an already concentrated position.

Main risk: the plan can become a comfort blanket. If the concentrated position rises, the investor may be tempted to delay; if it falls, the slow pace may leave too much unreduced risk.

What would change my judgment: evidence that the single-stock risk could threaten liquidity, retirement security, debt obligations, or required spending before the staged plan meaningfully reduces exposure. In that case, the diversification pace should accelerate despite the tax cost.
