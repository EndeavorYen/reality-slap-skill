I recommend proceeding with the staged diversification plan, provided it is treated as a binding risk-reduction program, not an excuse to keep the concentration indefinitely.

The material change is the objective: this is no longer “eliminate concentration risk as fast as possible.” It is “transition out of concentration while managing tax cost.” Given that, selling 6% of the portfolio per quarter, banning margin, and maintaining a cash reserve is a reasonable structure.

My boundaries would be:

- Do not add to the concentrated winner.
- Do not borrow against it.
- Keep the quarterly sale schedule preapproved and hard to override emotionally.
- Define tax budget, cash reserve target, and minimum diversification destination in advance.
- Add acceleration triggers if the position grows as a percentage of the portfolio, fundamentals deteriorate, liquidity needs change, or tax conditions improve.

So my recommendation changes from “reduce concentration directly and avoid margin” to “execute a disciplined tax-aware transition.” The no-margin rule remains non-negotiable. The plan is acceptable because it converts an unmanaged concentrated bet into a governed exit path.
