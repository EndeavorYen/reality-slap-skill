My stance: Disagree.

My recommendation: Do not buy now. Keep the diversified index position intact unless the investor first writes a thesis, defines position size, sets an exit rule, and can explain why this synthetic single-stock exposure is worth replacing diversified exposure.

Reasons:
- Price momentum and social-media excitement are not an investment thesis.
- A synthetic single stock can add leverage, tracking, liquidity, counterparty, or structure-specific risks.
- Selling diversified exposure to chase a three-day rally concentrates risk at exactly the moment discipline matters most.
- “This may be the only chance” is FOMO, not evidence.

Need to be careful: waiting may mean missing further upside. That is an acceptable cost compared with entering an undefined trade with no risk controls.

What would change my judgment: a written thesis, maximum loss limit, position size small enough to survive being wrong, clear exit criteria, and evidence beyond hype and recent price action. Until then, the default is no trade.
