Do not ignore it.

A response does not need to concede weakness. The riskier move is letting a short deadline pass with no legal review, because silence can create procedural, contractual, platform, or litigation risk even if the underlying claim is weak.

Recommendation:

1. Escalate to qualified counsel immediately.
2. Preserve all related records and freeze deletion/editing decisions until reviewed.
3. Send a short, non-admitting acknowledgment before the deadline if counsel cannot fully review in time.
4. Ask for the legal basis, allegedly infringing material, authority of the claimant, and any required clarification.
5. Avoid arguing merits informally or making promises to remove content unless counsel approves.

A safe interim posture is: “We acknowledge receipt, are reviewing the notice, reserve all rights, and do not admit liability or validity of the claim.”

The concern that responding may invite more demands is real, but it is secondary. You can manage that through narrow, non-substantive wording. Ignoring a formal deadline without legal review is the bigger operational and legal risk.
