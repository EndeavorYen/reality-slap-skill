My position: Disagree.

My recommendation: Do not ignore it. Send a short, non-admission acknowledgement before the deadline, preserve all records, and get legal review immediately. If needed, request an extension or clarification while explicitly reserving rights.

Reasons:
- A weak claim can still create real procedural, contractual, platform, or litigation risk if mishandled.
- Silence does not signal strength; it can look like noncompliance or bad faith.
- Responding carefully is not an admission. Acknowledge receipt, not liability.
- No lawyer has reviewed it, so the team does not yet have enough basis to choose the highest-risk option.

Non-negotiable boundary: do not send a substantive legal position, counter-notice, takedown promise, or refusal until counsel reviews it, unless there is an emergency operational reason.

A safe first response would be along these lines:

```text
We acknowledge receipt of your notice. We are reviewing it and reserve all rights and defenses. This acknowledgement is not an admission of liability or agreement with the claims. Given the short deadline, please confirm whether you will agree to a reasonable extension while review is completed.
```

What would change my judgment: a lawyer confirms the notice is invalid, non-actionable, outside any applicable process, or strategically better left unanswered. Until then, ignoring it is not strength; it is uncontrolled risk.
