I recommend **not moving the emergency reserve into the stock today**.

The key facts are unfavorable: the money may be needed within six months, the stock just moved sharply on social attention, and there is no new fundamental analysis. That makes this a liquidity and risk-management decision, not a momentum opportunity.

Keep the emergency reserve in cash or cash-like instruments. If the stock still fits the long-term plan after fresh analysis, consider using only pre-allocated investing capital, not emergency funds, and size it so a sharp reversal would not damage your financial stability.

The “rare chance before it is gone” feeling is exactly the condition where a cooling-off period helps. Missing a speculative move is survivable; losing needed emergency liquidity is not.
