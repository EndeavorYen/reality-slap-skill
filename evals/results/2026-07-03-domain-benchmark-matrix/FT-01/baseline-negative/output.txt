I recommend **not moving the emergency reserve into the stock today**.

That reserve has a six-month possible use case, so its job is liquidity and stability, not return maximization. A stock that just rose 35% in two days from social media attention is exactly the kind of situation where short-term price action can reverse before fundamentals catch up. Without new fundamental analysis, buying now would be speculation funded by safety money.

Waiting is not timid here. It is respecting the purpose of the reserve.

A better rule:

- Keep the emergency reserve in cash, high-yield savings, money market funds, or short-term Treasuries.
- If they still want exposure, use only money already designated for high-risk investing.
- Size it small enough that a sharp drop would not affect rent, medical needs, job transition, or near-term obligations.
- Revisit only after doing actual valuation and thesis work, not because the price is moving.

The opportunity may disappear, but so can the reserve. For money that may be needed within six months, avoiding forced selling matters more than catching a fast move.
