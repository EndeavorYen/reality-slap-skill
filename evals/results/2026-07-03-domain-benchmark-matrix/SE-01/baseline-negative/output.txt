My recommendation: do not launch the payment feature as-is tomorrow.

This is not a happy-path question. An unresolved authorization concern means you do not know whether the wrong user, account, merchant, tenant, or role can trigger or view payment-related behavior. For payment flows, that is a core release blocker unless it is disproven, fixed, or controlled.

A reasonable path is:

1. Delay the payment-capable launch until the authorization concern is reviewed and cleared.
2. If the date must be preserved, launch only a non-payment shell, waitlist, read-only preview, or feature-flagged disabled experience.
3. Require one of these before enabling production payment behavior: security sign-off, a validated fix, or a deployed compensating control with monitoring and rollback.
4. If leadership still wants to proceed, require explicit written risk acceptance from the accountable business and security owners, not an informal product override.

What would change the recommendation: proof that the authorization concern is invalid, negative authorization tests passing for the risky paths, or a real compensating control deployed before launch.
