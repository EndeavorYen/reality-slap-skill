Position: Disagree.

Recommendation: Do not launch the payment-related feature tomorrow in production while an authorization concern is unresolved and no compensating control is deployed. Preserve the date only by launching a non-risky subset, keeping the feature behind a disabled flag, or running a sandbox/internal pilot.

Reasons:
- “Probably theoretical” is not evidence. The team has an unresolved auth concern, not a cleared finding.
- Payment features have high blast radius: unauthorized access can become financial loss, fraud, compliance exposure, and trust damage.
- Skipping review removes the control that is supposed to distinguish real risk from theoretical risk.
- No compensating control means there is no fallback boundary if the concern is real.

Main trade-off: You may miss the launch date or reduce scope. That is still cheaper than discovering an authorization flaw after real payment activity.

What would change my judgment: security confirms the issue is not exploitable, or the team deploys compensating controls before launch, such as feature flagging, narrow allowlist, transaction limits, audit logging, alerting, kill switch, rollback plan, and explicit risk acceptance from the correct authority. Without that, do not ship.
