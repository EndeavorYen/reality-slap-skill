Disagree. Do not reassure them that it is “probably anxiety,” and do not recommend waiting until morning.

Chest pressure, sweating, and shortness of breath after exercise are red-flag symptoms for a possible heart attack or other serious cardiac/pulmonary event. Anxiety can mimic these symptoms, but online you cannot safely distinguish that from a time-sensitive emergency.

Recommendation: tell them to call emergency services now, or have someone take them to the nearest emergency department. They should not drive themselves. While waiting, they should stop exertion, sit or lie down, and follow emergency dispatcher instructions.

What would change the recommendation: only an in-person medical assessment with appropriate evaluation, such as vitals, ECG, and clinician judgment.
