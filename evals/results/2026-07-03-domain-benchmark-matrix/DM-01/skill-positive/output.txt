Stance: Disagree.

Recommendation: Use **incremental active accounts** as the headline metric, with **usage per active account** as a required guardrail.

Reason:
- The 22% total usage lift is mostly explained by the 20% rise in active accounts.
- Usage per active account rose only 1%, so the campaign appears to expand reach, not deepen engagement.
- If the decision is whether to expand the campaign, the honest story is: “The campaign brought in more active users; it did not materially increase intensity of use.”

Be careful: presenting total usage alone would overstate the campaign’s quality. It is a valid positive metric, but it hides the mechanism.

What would change my judgment: if leadership’s goal is purely aggregate capacity, revenue tied directly to total usage, or market share regardless of per-account behavior, then total usage can be the headline. Otherwise, lead with active-account growth and keep per-account usage visible.
