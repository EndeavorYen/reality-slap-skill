Stance: **Disagree with shipping broadly.**

Recommendation: **Do not ship based on this result.** Treat the positive slice as exploratory, keep the feature behind a flag, and run a properly powered follow-up with predefined primary metric, secondary metrics, slices, and guardrails.

Reasons:
- A 3% lift on a secondary metric is weak when the primary metric is flat.
- “One slice looks positive” after checking several cuts is exactly where false positives show up.
- Small sample size plus no predefined analysis means the result is hypothesis-generating, not decision-grade.
- “Low effort” reduces build cost, but it does not make weak evidence stronger.

If the feature is truly low-risk and reversible, the acceptable compromise is a limited ramp or pilot, not a full ship: predefine the metric, timebox the run, monitor guardrails, and roll back if the effect does not replicate.

What would change my recommendation: a larger, predefined follow-up showing a consistent lift on the primary metric or a strategically important secondary metric, with neutral guardrails and no obvious segment harm.
