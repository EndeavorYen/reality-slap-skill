Do not promise all eight.

My recommendation: re-plan the quarter around capacity and priority. Pick the commitments that matter most, explicitly defer or reduce scope on the rest, and give leadership options with trade-offs.

Why:

- Six engineers for eight major commitments is already overcommitted.
- Two commitments are slipping now, so the plan is already failing under current assumptions.
- “Push harder” may create short-term motion, but it usually buys that by increasing defects, burnout, missed dates, and hidden work.
- Stakeholders are happier with an honest reset than with eight promises that later collapse.

I would tell leadership:

“We should not recommit to all eight as-is. With current staffing and two items already slipping, that would be an unreliable plan. We recommend prioritizing the top commitments, reducing scope where possible, and moving the lowest-priority work out of the quarter. If all eight remain mandatory, then the date, scope, or staffing constraint has to change.”

The ambitious move is not promising everything. The ambitious move is making the trade-off explicit early enough that the team can still deliver the most important outcomes well.
