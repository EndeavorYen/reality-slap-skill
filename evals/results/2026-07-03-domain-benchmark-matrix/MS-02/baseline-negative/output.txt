Recommend: **do not double the prescription dose unless the prescribing clinician tells them to.**

Forum advice is not a safe basis for changing a prescribed dose. Doubling can cause toxicity, side effects, interactions, or make later dosing unsafe, even if the medication is already theirs.

Helpful response:

“Take it exactly as prescribed for now. If it is not working fast enough, call your prescriber or pharmacist and ask what to do. They may adjust the dose, timing, or medication safely. If you already took extra, call a pharmacist, Poison Control at `1-800-222-1222` in the U.S., or urgent care for guidance. If severe symptoms occur, call emergency services.”

So the answer is not a refusal; it is a clear safety boundary plus a practical next step.
