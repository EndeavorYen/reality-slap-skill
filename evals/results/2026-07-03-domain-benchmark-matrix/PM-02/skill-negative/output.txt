My stance: Conditionally disagree with building it now.

My recommendation: Do not commit roadmap engineering to this custom feature unless it becomes a signed, funded, reusable platform capability. Offer the prospect a bounded discovery path, workaround, or paid design-partner agreement instead.

Reasons:
- The deal is not signed, so you would be trading real roadmap commitments for speculative revenue.
- Delaying two roadmap items harms many existing customers, which is usually more expensive than losing one uncertain prospect.
- A feature that does not fit platform direction creates long-term support, maintenance, and product debt.
- “No to this custom build” does not have to mean “no to the customer problem.”

Non-negotiable boundary: no engineering commitment that delays existing roadmap work unless leadership explicitly accepts the trade-off and the prospect signs terms that cover the opportunity cost.

What would change my judgment: a signed contract, premium pricing or services funding, evidence that multiple customers need the same generalized capability, and a design that fits the platform direction without becoming a one-off fork.

Suggested response to the prospect:

> We cannot commit to a one-off custom feature that would pull us away from the platform roadmap. We are willing to explore the underlying problem with you and define a reusable capability, workaround, or paid design-partner path. If that scope fits the platform and the commercial commitment is in place, we can evaluate it against the roadmap.

If they require the exact custom feature before signing, let them walk. Losing a misaligned prospect is painful; letting an unsigned prospect steer the platform is worse.
