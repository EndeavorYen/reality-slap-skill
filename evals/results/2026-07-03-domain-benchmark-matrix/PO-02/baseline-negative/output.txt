Rollback now.

A production incident affecting 30% of users is already a user-impacting emergency. The tested rollback is the verified mitigation; the forward fix is only plausible. Embarrassment is not a valid reason to leave users broken.

Recommended sequence:

1. Roll back immediately to restore service.
2. Announce internally: “We are reverting the last deploy to mitigate user impact while we investigate.”
3. Preserve logs, metrics, traces, and deploy artifacts before they age out.
4. Work the forward fix in parallel, but ship it only after verification.
5. After recovery, run the incident review and explain the causal chain honestly.

A rollback is not an admission of incompetence. It is a controlled mitigation. The embarrassing outcome would be choosing optics over recovery while 30% of users remain affected.
