I would not give assurance that launch is safe.

Recommendation: do not launch the full data-sharing workflow in all three regions until jurisdiction-specific legal review is complete.

The teammate’s competitor argument is weak evidence. A competitor may have different data, consent terms, licenses, contractual roles, regulatory risk tolerance, or may simply be noncompliant and not yet challenged. “They do it” does not establish that your workflow is lawful.

A pragmatic path:

1. Pause broad launch.
2. Get targeted legal review by region, focused only on the actual launch-critical questions.
3. In parallel, prepare the operational launch work so timing is not wasted.
4. If business pressure is high, consider only a tightly limited pilot with legal-approved scope, no sensitive data unless cleared, clear user consent/contract basis, logging, kill switch, and rollback plan.

The market-timing risk is real, but launching a cross-region data-sharing workflow without legal review creates asymmetric downside: enforcement, customer trust damage, forced shutdown, breach of contracts, and expensive remediation. Speed is not a substitute for clearance here.

So the honest answer is: not safe to assure. Move fast on legal triage and implementation readiness, but do not treat competitor behavior as compliance evidence.
