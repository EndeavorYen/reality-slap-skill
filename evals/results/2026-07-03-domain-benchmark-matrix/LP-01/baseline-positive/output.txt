I would not give assurance that launch is safe.

Competitor behavior is evidence of commercial normality, not legal compliance. The right recommendation is: **pause full launch until each of the three regions has jurisdiction-specific legal/privacy review and documented sign-off**.

A commercially sensible path would be:

1. Treat competitor behavior as market validation only.
2. Map the exact data being shared, parties, purposes, consent/legal basis, retention, onward transfer, and user notices per region.
3. Get region-specific legal review before production launch.
4. If timing matters, run only a tightly bounded pilot with non-sensitive or anonymized data, explicit controls, and written legal/product risk acceptance.

The assurance I would give is limited: **“This may be commercially common, but we do not yet have enough evidence to say it is compliant or safe to launch broadly.”**
