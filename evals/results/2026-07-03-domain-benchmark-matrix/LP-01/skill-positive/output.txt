My stance: Disagree.

My recommendation: do not treat the launch as safe. Competitor behavior is a market signal, not compliance evidence. Hold production data sharing across the three regions until you have jurisdiction-specific legal/privacy review and an explicit launch gate.

Reasons:
- A competitor may have different data, consent, contracts, residency setup, licenses, or risk tolerance.
- Data-sharing rules can change by region, data category, purpose, recipient, and cross-border transfer path.
- Without review, you cannot credibly assess notices, legal basis, user rights, retention, security, processor/controller roles, or transfer mechanisms.
- The downside is asymmetric: a bad launch can trigger shutdown, remediation, customer trust loss, regulator scrutiny, or contract breach.

If speed matters, run only a reversible pilot: synthetic/anonymized data, one reviewed region, limited partners, logging, kill switch, and no expansion until counsel signs off.

What would change my judgment: a region-by-region legal assessment confirming the workflow, required controls, responsible owners, residual risks, and written approval for launch.
