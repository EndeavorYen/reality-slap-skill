My stance: Disagree.

My recommendation: do not launch the full three-region data-sharing workflow until each region has jurisdiction-specific legal review. Protect timing by running legal review in parallel with engineering readiness, and only proceed with reversible, low-risk work: synthetic-data testing, internal dry runs, feature flags, audit logging, kill switch, data mapping, and a reviewed limited pilot if legal approves it.

Reasons:
- “A competitor does it” is not evidence of compliance; their data, consents, contracts, architecture, and risk tolerance may differ.
- Three regions means three legal regimes. One unreviewed assumption can create regulatory, contractual, or customer-trust damage.
- You cannot honestly assure launch safety without knowing what data is shared, who receives it, legal basis, retention, onward transfer, user notice, and rollback controls.

Non-negotiable boundary: do not share real customer or personal data in any unreviewed jurisdiction.

What would change my judgment: written legal approval per region, a reviewed data-flow map, confirmed lawful basis/consent or contract terms, transfer controls, retention/deletion rules, and an operational rollback plan.
