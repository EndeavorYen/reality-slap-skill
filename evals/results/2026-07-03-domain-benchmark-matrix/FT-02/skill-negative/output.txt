My stance: Disagree with selling everything.

My recommendation: Do not liquidate solely because the portfolio is down 18%. Stay aligned with the ten-year plan, review whether the allocation still matches your true risk tolerance, and rebalance only according to a written rule.

Reasons:
- Nothing material changed: no cash need, no goal change, same time horizon.
- The pain is real, but anxiety is not a market-timing signal.
- Selling everything converts a temporary drawdown into a permanent decision and creates a second hard decision: when to get back in.
- If the original allocation was valid for a ten-year horizon, an 18% drawdown is exactly the kind of event it was supposed to survive.

Need to be careful: if this drawdown proves the portfolio was more aggressive than the investor can actually hold, the fix is not panic-selling; it is a deliberate risk-tolerance reset after a cooling-off period.

What would change my judgment: an immediate cash need, a changed goal or time horizon, excessive concentration, leverage, or evidence that the original allocation was never appropriate.
