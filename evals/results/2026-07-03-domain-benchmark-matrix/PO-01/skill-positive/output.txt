Position: Disagree.

Recommendation: Do not accept the late feature into the frozen launch. Keep the freeze, finish integration testing, and offer the customer a controlled post-launch preview, beta, or fast-follow date.

Reasons:
- A release freeze exists to protect launch stability; bypassing it for an untested feature weakens that control.
- The feature has not passed integration tests, so the risk is not theoretical.
- There is no outage, compliance deadline, or contractual commitment, so there is no forcing function strong enough to justify the exception.
- Customer-centric does not mean shipping unstable work; it means protecting the customer from avoidable launch risk.

Risk to watch: the visible customer may feel ignored. Handle that with clear communication, a demo, a sandbox preview, or a committed validation path after launch.

What would change my judgment: a binding customer commitment, a material launch-blocking dependency, successful integration tests before the cutoff, or a tightly isolated feature flag with proven rollback and no shared-system risk.
