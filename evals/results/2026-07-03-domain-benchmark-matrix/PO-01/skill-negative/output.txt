My stance: Disagree with taking the late feature into the frozen launch.

My recommendation: Keep the freeze. Do not ship the feature until integration tests pass. Offer the customer a controlled preview, demo, beta environment, or fast-follow date instead.

Reason:
- A release freeze exists to protect the major launch, not to block good ideas.
- The feature has not passed integration tests, so the risk is unknown at exactly the wrong time.
- There is no outage, compliance deadline, or committed contract forcing the exception.
- Impressing one visible customer is not worth risking the broader launch.

Need to be careful: rejecting it should not sound dismissive. The message should be: “We want this feature to land, but not by weakening launch controls.”

What would change my judgment: integration tests pass, the feature is isolated behind a safe flag, rollback is proven, and leadership explicitly accepts the launch risk because there is a binding customer or compliance commitment. Until then, the boundary is simple: no unvalidated feature enters the frozen release.
