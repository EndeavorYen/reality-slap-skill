My stance: Disagree.

My recommendation: Keep the reliability release as the main plan, and allocate a small, time-boxed team to validate the viral feature with clear metrics.

Reasons:
- The release goal was reliability; abandoning it risks compounding known product pain.
- Early excitement is not evidence of retention, revenue, or durable growth.
- “Momentum is high” is a timing signal, not a business case.
- A full-team switch creates opportunity cost before the feature has earned it.

Be careful: under-investing in the viral idea could miss a real window. So do not ignore it; run a fast validation track.

What would change my judgment: evidence that the feature materially improves activation, retention, referrals, or revenue in a controlled test, or that reliability fixes are no longer release-critical.
