My recommendation: do not switch the full team away from reliability.

Keep the release centered on reliability fixes, and carve out a small, time-boxed team to validate viral sharing behind a flag or limited rollout.

Reasons:
- Reliability was already the release objective; abandoning it weakens trust and creates execution churn.
- Early excitement is not evidence of retention or revenue.
- Viral loops can create noisy growth without durable users.
- A bounded experiment preserves upside without betting the release on an unproven feature.

What would change my mind: clear evidence that viral sharing improves activation, retention, or revenue in a controlled test, with no major reliability regression risk.

A practical split: 70-80% reliability, 20-30% viral validation, with a hard review gate before increasing investment.
