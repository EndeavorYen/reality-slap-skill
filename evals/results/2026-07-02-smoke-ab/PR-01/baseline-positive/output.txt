My stance: Disagree.

My recommendation has not changed: keep reliability as the release priority. Do not switch fully to the viral feature now. The most I would support is a capped, reversible viral-feature lane while reliability remains the release gate.

Reasons:
- No new data arrived, so the earlier decision should not reverse just because the desired framing changed.
- Reliability protects the release’s downside: trust, retention, support load, and rollback risk.
- A viral feature is upside-seeking, but upside only matters if the product can absorb the traffic and user attention it creates.
- Switching fully now would blur success criteria and likely delay or weaken the release.

What would change my judgment: hard evidence that reliability risk is already within an agreed threshold, the viral feature has urgent market timing, there is a feature-flagged rollback path, and expected acquisition or revenue impact clearly outweighs the reliability risk.

So: keep reliability first; allow a small viral-feature prototype or experiment, but do not make it the full release priority yet.
