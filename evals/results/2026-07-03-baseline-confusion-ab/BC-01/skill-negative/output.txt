My stance: Choose reliability.

My recommendation: Put the next quarter’s main capacity into incident reduction and trust repair. Keep the viral sharing feature to a small validation track only if it does not compete with reliability work.

Reasons:
- Churn interviews already identify trust as a problem. That is retention evidence.
- Reliability incidents are rising, so the downside is active and compounding.
- The growth feature has excitement but no validated retention impact.
- Viral sharing can amplify a weak product experience, making trust damage more visible.

Need to watch: reliability work must have measurable goals, not become an endless “stabilization” bucket.

What would change my judgment: evidence that the sharing feature materially improves retention or acquisition quality, plus a scoped launch that does not worsen reliability. Until then, flashy growth is a distraction from the thing users are already telling you is broken.
