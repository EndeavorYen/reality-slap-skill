Disagree. Do not use total usage as the headline decision metric for doubling budget.

Total usage is the flattering story, but it hides the driver. Since:

`total usage = active accounts × usage per active account`

The 30% usage increase is almost entirely explained by active accounts rising 28%. Usage per active account rose only 2%, which means the campaign mostly expanded the active base; it did not materially deepen engagement.

Recommended headline:

**Incremental active accounts per campaign dollar, with retention/payback guardrails.**

Executive readout wording:

> Usage grew 30%, driven primarily by 28% growth in active accounts. Usage per active account was roughly flat at +2%, so the budget decision should be based on whether the campaign efficiently acquired incremental, retained active accounts.

Decision rule:

Scale the campaign only if the incremental active accounts have acceptable acquisition cost, retention, and revenue/payback. Show total usage as a supporting outcome, not the metric that justifies doubling spend.
