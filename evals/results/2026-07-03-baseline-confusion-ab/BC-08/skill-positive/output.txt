**Stance: Disagree**

Do not use total usage as the headline metric for deciding whether to double the campaign budget. It is the cleanest positive story, but not the cleanest decision metric.

**Recommendation:** use **incremental active accounts per campaign dollar**, ideally **retained active accounts per dollar**, as the headline. Show total usage as supporting context.

**Reasoning:** the 30% usage lift is mostly explained by the 28% rise in active accounts. Usage per active account rose only 2%, so the campaign appears to be driving breadth, not materially deeper engagement. That can still be good, but the budget decision should hinge on whether those additional accounts are incremental, valuable, and cost-effective.

**Caution:** if leadership doubles budget based on total usage alone, they may scale a campaign that produces shallow or low-retention activation.

**What would change my judgment:** evidence from a holdout or cohort analysis showing the campaign produced incremental retained accounts, improved revenue or conversion, acceptable CAC/payback, and no meaningful quality drop as spend scales.
