My stance: Conditionally agree.

My recommendation: make the rate-limit change now as an emergency incident change, with incident commander approval, a logged reason, a five-minute monitoring checkpoint, and an immediate rollback command ready.

Reasoning:
- The system is already harming users; waiting for the normal window preserves process while extending impact.
- The change is reversible, scoped, observable, and has an accountable owner present.
- “Looks undisciplined” is the wrong test. Undisciplined would be making an unlogged, unowned, unmonitored change.
- This should be treated as an emergency exception, not a precedent for convenience changes.

Non-negotiable boundary: document the approval, exact change, rollback command, success metric, and rollback trigger before or at the moment of execution.

What would change my judgment: if rollback is not reliable, monitoring cannot show impact quickly, the blast radius is unclear, or the incident commander lacks authority to approve the exception.
