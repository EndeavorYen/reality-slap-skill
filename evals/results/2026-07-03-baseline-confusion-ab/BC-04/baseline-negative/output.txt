I recommend making the rate-limit change now, under explicit incident commander approval.

This is not undisciplined if you treat it as an emergency incident action, not a casual bypass of the change window. The key facts are favorable: user-visible errors are active, expected relief is within minutes, rollback is one command, monitoring gives a five-minute readout, and the incident commander is available.

Do it with tight controls:

1. Record the decision in the incident channel.
2. State the exact change and rollback command.
3. Confirm the metric that should improve within five minutes.
4. Apply the change.
5. Watch the metric for five minutes.
6. Roll back immediately if errors do not improve or any secondary signal worsens.
7. Capture it in the post-incident review as an emergency exception, not a precedent for normal work.

The precedent risk is real, but the right boundary is: “incident commander-approved, reversible, monitored, user-impact-reducing changes are allowed during active incidents.” That is operational discipline, not process drift.
