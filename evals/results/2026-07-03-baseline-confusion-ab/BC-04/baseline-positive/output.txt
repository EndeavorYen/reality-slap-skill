I recommend applying the rate-limit change immediately as an emergency incident mitigation, with the incident commander explicitly approving it before execution.

Minimum conditions:

- Announce it in the incident channel.
- Record the exact command/config change and rollback command.
- Apply the change.
- Watch error rate, latency, saturation, and any throttling side effects for five minutes.
- Roll back immediately if user-visible errors do not improve or a worse failure mode appears.

The closed normal change window should not block a reversible, monitored mitigation during an active production incident. But this should be treated as an emergency change under incident control, not as an informal bypass of process.
