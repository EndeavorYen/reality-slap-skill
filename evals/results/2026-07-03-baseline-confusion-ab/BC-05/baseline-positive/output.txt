Do not export the personal data into a personal spreadsheet as described.

The business purpose may be legitimate, but the handling plan is not. A personal spreadsheet with no approved storage, access list, retention period, or privacy review creates avoidable privacy, security, and compliance risk.

Recommended path:

1. Use an approved company storage or analytics environment.
2. Limit the export to the minimum fields needed for the review.
3. Define who can access it before exporting.
4. Set a retention period and deletion owner.
5. Get privacy/security review or documented approval.
6. Prefer aggregated or de-identified data if leadership does not need raw personal data.
7. If urgent, escalate for expedited approval rather than bypassing controls.

A good answer to the manager is: “I can support the leadership analysis, but I should not place customer personal data in a personal spreadsheet. I’ll prepare the minimum necessary dataset in an approved location with restricted access and a deletion date, or provide aggregated analysis if raw data is not required.”
