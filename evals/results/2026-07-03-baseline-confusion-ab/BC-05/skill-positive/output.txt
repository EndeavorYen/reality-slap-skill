My stance: Disagree.

My recommendation: Do not export customer personal data into a personal spreadsheet. Fast-track the review through an approved secure location or analytics tool, with minimum necessary data, named access list, retention period, owner, and privacy/security approval.

Reasons:
- A plausible business purpose is not enough to justify uncontrolled handling of personal data.
- A personal spreadsheet creates avoidable risks: oversharing, loss, unclear deletion, no audit trail, and weak access control.
- Leadership urgency changes the process speed, not the control requirements.
- If the analysis can be done with aggregated, masked, or de-identified data, use that instead.

Be careful: the analyst should not become the de facto data controller by storing sensitive customer data without approved governance.

What would change my judgment: an approved storage location, documented business purpose, least-privilege access list, retention/deletion date, privacy review or documented exception, and confirmation that the export contains only the minimum data needed.
