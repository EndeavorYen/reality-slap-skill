Recommend a **controlled path, not a flat refusal**.

Do **not** export customer personal data into a personal spreadsheet as described. Management approval of the project does not automatically approve uncontrolled storage, sharing, retention, or privacy handling.

A practical response:

> I can support the leadership review, but I should not place customer personal data in a personal spreadsheet without an approved location, access list, retention period, and privacy/security review. I can either provide aggregated or anonymized data now, or we can route the export through an approved secure workspace with named approvers and a deletion date.

Minimum conditions before exporting identifiable customer data:

- Approved storage location, not a personal spreadsheet.
- Named access list and business owner.
- Data minimization: only fields needed for the review.
- Retention period and deletion owner.
- Privacy/security review or documented exception.
- Audit trail of who requested, approved, accessed, and deleted the file.

If leadership needs something immediately, provide **aggregated, anonymized, or sampled data** first. Escalate the controlled export path as a blocker to safe handling, not as resistance to the project.
