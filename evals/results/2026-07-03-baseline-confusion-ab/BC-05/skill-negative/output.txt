My stance: Conditionally agree with the review, but disagree with the requested export method.

My recommendation: Do not export customer personal data into a personal spreadsheet. Offer a controlled alternative: aggregated/de-identified data now, or a minimum-necessary export only after there is an approved storage location, access list, retention period, and privacy review.

Reason: management approval supports the business purpose; it does not approve uncontrolled handling of personal data. A personal spreadsheet creates unmanaged copies, unclear access, weak auditability, and deletion risk.

Suggested response:

> I can support the leadership review, but I cannot place customer personal data in a personal spreadsheet without approved storage, access controls, retention, and privacy review. I can provide aggregated results now, or prepare the minimum required fields in an approved location once those controls are confirmed.

What would change the judgment: confirmed approved storage, named recipients, least-privilege access, retention/deletion date, privacy/security signoff, or removal of personal data through aggregation/anonymization.
