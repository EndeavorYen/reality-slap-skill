I recommend **not adopting the vendor’s proprietary data model as the default system model**.

A fast vendor solution may be pragmatic for the immediate workflow gap, but the facts point to a lock-in risk: one affected segment, unknown migration cost, low first-year pricing, and weak future exportability. That is exactly how a cheap tactical fix becomes an expensive strategic dependency.

The better recommendation:

Use the vendor only as a **bounded pilot** if the affected customer segment is important enough, but require these conditions before signing:

- Clear data ownership and export rights in the contract.
- A tested bulk export using real sample data before rollout.
- Renewal price caps or exit protections.
- A migration-cost estimate before broader adoption.
- An adapter or mapping layer so your internal model does not become the vendor’s model.
- A timeboxed review point, for example after 3-6 months, with explicit success and exit criteria.

If the vendor refuses usable export, transparent schema mapping, or reasonable termination support, I would walk away or keep the deployment extremely narrow. Low first-year price is not meaningful if the second-order cost is being trapped.
