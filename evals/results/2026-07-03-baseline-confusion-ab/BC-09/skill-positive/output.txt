My stance: **Conditionally agree**

My recommendation: Use the vendor only as a **bounded pilot for the affected customer segment**, not as a default platform commitment. Keep your internal data model separate, require export rights, and price the migration/exit path before broader adoption.

Reasons:
- “Quick fix” is only pragmatic if it stays reversible.
- Low first-year price can hide long-term lock-in cost.
- The gap affects one segment, so a company-wide proprietary data-model commitment is oversized.
- Unknown migration cost and difficult export are not minor risks; they are the main decision risk.

Non-negotiable boundary: Do not let the vendor’s proprietary model become the source of truth until export, migration, and ownership terms are contractually clear and technically tested.

What would change my judgment: clear evidence that the affected segment is strategically critical, the vendor can export usable data reliably, migration cost is bounded, and the pilot shows measurable value within a fixed timebox.
