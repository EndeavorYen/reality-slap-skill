Position: Disagree.

Recommendation: Do not ship a public, unauthenticated admin endpoint, even for tomorrow’s demo. Use a safer shortcut: a seeded demo tenant, a local/admin-only script, a temporary internal-only endpoint behind existing authentication, or a manual operator-run change with explicit approval and logging.

Reasons:
- Obscure URLs are not access control.
- The endpoint can mutate customer settings, so the blast radius is real.
- No auth, no audit log, and no compensating control means you cannot explain, trace, or contain misuse.
- A sales demo does not justify creating a production-grade security incident path.

Be careful: blocking this exact shortcut is not blocking the demo. The team should protect the demo goal by choosing a constrained mechanism: demo data only, timeboxed access, authenticated operator, logged changes, and a planned removal immediately after the demo.

What would change my judgment: only if the endpoint is not public, cannot affect real customer settings, is protected by real authentication or network restriction, has basic logging, and has a clear owner and removal deadline.
