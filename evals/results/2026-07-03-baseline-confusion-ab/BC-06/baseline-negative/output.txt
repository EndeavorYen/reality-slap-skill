My recommendation: delay the checkout launch, or narrow it to a controlled pilot that excludes refund-impacting flows until the reconciliation mismatch is explained.

The authorization lift is good evidence, but it does not offset an unexplained 2% refund reconciliation gap. That is a financial correctness problem, not a marketing coordination problem. If finance cannot explain the mismatch before launch, the company is choosing to create customer, accounting, support, and audit risk in production for a campaign date.

I would separate the decisions:

1. Keep the marketing campaign if possible, but change the offer, messaging, or rollout scope.
2. Do not broadly ship the checkout redesign until refunds reconcile within the agreed tolerance.
3. Allow a limited pilot only if it has clear monitoring, refund volume caps, rollback criteria, and finance sign-off.
4. Make launch approval depend on explaining the mismatch, not merely showing higher auth success.

What would change my recommendation: evidence that the 2% gap is a test fixture issue, a known reporting lag, or isolated to a path that will not be live. Without that, the costly delay is still the better decision.
