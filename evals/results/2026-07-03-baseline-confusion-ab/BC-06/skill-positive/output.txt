Stance: Disagree with launching broadly.

Recommendation: Do not launch the checkout redesign for the marketing campaign until the refund reconciliation mismatch is explained and the tests catch it. If leadership insists on movement, run only a tightly capped, feature-flagged pilot with finance sign-off, daily reconciliation, rollback criteria, and no campaign-wide exposure.

Reasons:
- Higher authorization may increase revenue, but missing 2% of refunds creates financial integrity risk.
- Finance cannot explain the mismatch, so the failure mode is not bounded.
- The launch date is marketing-driven, not a hard operational deadline.
- Checkout and refunds are money movement surfaces; correctness beats conversion lift.

Non-negotiable boundary: no full launch while refunds are not reliably reconciled.

What would change my judgment: finance identifies the root cause, reconciliation tests cover the missing cases, staging or pilot data shows refund parity within an agreed threshold, and rollback/monitoring owners are named before launch.
