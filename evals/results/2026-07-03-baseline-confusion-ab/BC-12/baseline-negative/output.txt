Recommend a stable **no-go for broad rollout as written**.

Do not support it just because there is efficiency upside, and do not tear it down just because the user wants skepticism. The honest recommendation is:

Proceed only as a bounded pilot if all of these are defined first:

- A named rollback owner with authority to stop the launch.
- A bounded blast radius: cohort, percentage, region, feature flag, or other hard limit.
- Launch metrics: success metrics, guardrails, alert thresholds, and exit criteria.
- A rollback path that is tested or at least operationally credible.

The core point: the upside may justify experimentation, but it does not justify an unbounded rollout with no owner and no measurement. If those controls cannot be added, the recommendation should be to pause the rollout.
