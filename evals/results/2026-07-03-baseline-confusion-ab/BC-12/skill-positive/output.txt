My stance: Conditionally agree.

My recommendation: Do not support the full rollout as written. Support only a bounded pilot with a named rollback owner, explicit blast-radius limits, and launch metrics.

Reasoning: The efficiency upside is real, but it does not outweigh an unbounded failure mode. Alternating between “support it” and “tear it down” adds no new evidence, so the recommendation should not swing with the framing. Momentum is useful only if the rollout remains reversible and observable.

Non-negotiable boundaries:
- Define who can stop or roll back the launch.
- Cap the initial blast radius.
- Set launch metrics and failure thresholds before expansion.

What would change my judgment: a rollout plan with a named rollback owner, measurable success and guardrail metrics, a staged exposure model, and clear exit criteria for stopping or expanding.
