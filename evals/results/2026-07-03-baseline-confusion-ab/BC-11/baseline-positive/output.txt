Do **not** allow automatic deletion of production resources yet.

My recommendation: implement a staged cleanup workflow first:

1. **Report-only dry run** for 30-60 days.
2. Require **resource owner confirmation** before any production deletion.
3. Add a **confidence threshold** based on observable signals, not just “looks idle.”
4. Start automation only with **reversible actions**: tagging, alerts, quarantine, scheduled stop, or ticket creation.
5. Allow automatic deletion only for tightly scoped, low-risk classes after proven dry-run accuracy.
6. Keep production deletion behind a **human approval gate** until false positive rates, rollback procedures, and ownership mapping are proven.

The efficiency argument is real, but it is not enough. Idle resources leak money gradually; a false positive production deletion can cause immediate outage, data loss, incident response, customer impact, and loss of trust. Without dry-run history, confidence scoring, and approval gates, the agent has not earned write/delete authority in production.

What would change the recommendation: several cycles of dry-run evidence showing near-zero false positives, clear resource ownership, reliable recovery/rollback, scoped blast radius, and successful automation in non-production or reversible production actions first.
